About sRNA-TAPS

sRNA-TAPS was developed at the Institute of Chemical Epigenetics Munich (ICEM), Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität München, to bring TAPS-based 5mC and 5hmC detection to the world of small RNA.

The project

TAPS was originally developed for double-stranded DNA methylation mapping. Adapting it for small RNA presented unique challenges — short reads, strand specificity, extreme biotype diversity, and the absence of suitable spike-in controls for conversion efficiency calibration.

sRNA-TAPS addresses these challenges through TAPS-aware alignment parameters, a custom multi-mapper-aware methylation caller, three-layer SNP filtering, and a three-condition experimental framework that uses pyridine-borane-only treated samples for position-specific background correction.
