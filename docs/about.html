About sRNA-TAPS

sRNA-TAPS was developed by Dr. Bennett Henzeler in the Schneider Lab at the Institute of Chemical Epigenetics Munich (ICEM), Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität München, as part of ongoing research into the chemical and functional landscape of RNA modifications. The pipeline addresses a gap in the field — while TAPS-based sequencing has transformed DNA methylation mapping, its application to small RNA remained unexplored. Small RNA molecules present unique analytical challenges: they are short, strand-specific, highly repetitive across gene families, and degrade readily under the harsh chemical conditions that bisulfite sequencing requires. sRNA-TAPS was built from the ground up to handle these challenges, combining TAPS-aware alignment, multi-mapper fractional weighting, biotype-resolved calling, and a three-condition experimental framework that uses chemistry controls rather than synthetic spike-ins for background correction. The result is a pipeline that detects m5C and 5hmC across seven RNA biotypes — miRNA, tRNA, rRNA, snoRNA, snRNA, piRNA, and lncRNA — directly from human small RNA sequencing data.
