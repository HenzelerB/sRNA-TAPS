About sRNA-TAPS

sRNA-TAPS is an open-source analysis pipeline developed in the Schneider Lab at the Institute of Chemical Epigenetics Munich (ICEM), LMU Munich. It adapts TET-assisted pyridine borane sequencing (TAPS) to study cytosine modifications in small RNA, where short, repetitive, and strand-specific molecules create distinct analytical challenges.

Built specifically for small-RNA data, the workflow combines TAPS-aware alignment, fractional handling of multi-mapping reads, biotype-resolved modification calling, and a three-condition framework for chemistry-based background correction. It supports analysis of m5C and hm5C across miRNA, tRNA, rRNA, snoRNA, snRNA, piRNA, and lncRNA in human samples, without the harsh degradation associated with bisulfite treatment.

Designed for Small-RNA complexity

TAPS-aware alignment is adapted to short, repetitive, and strand-specific molecules.

Built around Reliable signal correction

A three-condition framework supports chemistry-aware background correction from the start.

Covers Multiple RNA biotypes

Modification calling is resolved across miRNA, tRNA, rRNA, snoRNA, snRNA, piRNA, and lncRNA.
