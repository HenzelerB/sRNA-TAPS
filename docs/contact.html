FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What sequencing depth do I need for reliable m5C detection?

Minimum coverage thresholds vary by biotype: rRNA requires ≥10x, miRNA and snoRNA ≥5x, and tRNA ≥3x. In practice, deeper coverage improves sensitivity and reduces false positives. For miRNA, we recommend at least 20–30x coverage at expressed loci. Standard small RNA-seq libraries of 10–20 million reads per sample are generally sufficient.

Can I use sRNA-TAPS without a pb_Ctrl condition?

The pipeline requires a pb_Ctrl condition for background correction. Without it, the delta (δ) score cannot be calculated, and genuine m5C signal cannot be distinguished from pyridine borane chemistry noise (~8–9% background C→T). We strongly recommend including at least two pb_Ctrl replicates per cell line in your experimental design.

Why are most of my reads assigned to the "other" biotype?

The "other" category captures reads that do not overlap a uniquely annotated Ensembl feature, or that map to regions where multiple biotypes share equal priority in the annotation hierarchy. This is common in small RNA-seq data and does not indicate a pipeline problem.

Several factors can contribute to a high "other" fraction: reads mapping to unannotated intergenic regions, reads overlapping antisense transcripts or pseudogenes not classified under the main biotypes, and multi-mapping reads that cannot be confidently assigned to a single locus. In TAPS-treated samples, the chemical conversion step can also affect alignment scores at modified positions, occasionally pushing reads into ambiguous mapping territory.

To reduce the "other" fraction, ensure you are using a current Ensembl GTF for your selected species and release, that your alignment is to the full genome rather than a transcriptome, and that your GTF biotype priority list covers the RNA species most relevant to your experiment.

My alignment rate is very low — what should I check?

First check that adapter trimming ran correctly — untrimmed reads align poorly. Verify that you are using --norc (forward strand only) and -v 2 in Bowtie1. If your alignment rate is below 60%, check the trimming report for adapter content and read length distribution. For synthetic test data, ~80% alignment is expected; for real data, 70–90% is typical depending on library quality.

Can I run sRNA-TAPS on mouse or other non-human genomes?

Yes. sRNA-TAPS now supports species-aware reference setup, with built-in Ensembl-backed configurations for human, mouse, rat, zebrafish, fruit fly, c_elegans, and chicken, plus custom FASTA/GTF inputs for other organisms. For non-human runs, use the matching genome FASTA, annotation, and any species-appropriate variant resources you need for filtering.

How do I interpret a negative delta (δ) score?

A negative δ means the pb_Ctrl mod_rate is higher than the treat mod_rate at that position. This typically indicates a SNP or sequence variant that causes elevated C→T in the background control, or a position where pyridine borane chemistry has unusual context-specific reactivity. Sites with δ < 0 are filtered out during the differential methylation step and should not be interpreted as biologically meaningful.

Why does the report show empty benchmarking figures?

Benchmarking figures require the 09.compare/ directory to be populated, which in turn requires the benchmarking tools (rastair, asTair, Bismark) to have been run in step 08. If you ran the pipeline without the all_benchmark target, these steps are skipped. Re-run Snakemake with the all_benchmark target to include benchmarking — see the Tutorial for the full command.

How do I cite sRNA-TAPS?

Please cite: Simsir Ö., Henzeler B. et al. sRNA-TAPS: TAPS-based m5C detection in small RNA. (in preparation, 2026). See the References page for a full list of tools and methods used in the pipeline that should also be cited.
